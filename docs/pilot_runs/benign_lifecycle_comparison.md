## Lifecycle Findings

- All compared traces are valid under the current normalized event-order
  validator.
- Streaming traces from all three providers expose enough raw events to map
  `first_byte`, `first_token`, `chunk`, `stream_end`, `final_response`,
  `iterator_end`, and `settled` as distinct normalized lifecycle events.
- Non-streaming traces do not have a provider stream boundary, so
  `stream_end` is not applicable; they still separate `final_response`,
  `iterator_end`, and `settled` in the harness.
- Terminal reasons are influenced by the conservative
  `max_output_tokens=128` pilot limit. `length` here is a harness-limit
  observation, not a provider safety signal.

## P3 Decision

P2.M4 is sufficient to complete P3.M1 policy scaffolding: redacted
safety-category policy, prompt governance, and safety-signal trace
contracts. It is not sufficient to start P3.M2 safety-signal pilot
runs. P3.M2 should remain blocked until prompt sourcing, category labels,
review policy, and output-retention rules are reviewed.
